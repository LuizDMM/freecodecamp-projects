Machado de assis

The most important Portuguese-speaking author of all time

Here's a time line of Machado de Assis' life

  • 1839 - Born in Rio de Janeiro, on June 21, Joaquim Maria Machado de Assis, son of Brazilian Francisco José de Assis and Azorean Maria Leopoldina Machado de Assis, residents of Morro do Livramento.
  • 1849 - After the death of his mother and his only sister, Machado is supported by his godmother.
  • 1855 - He publishes his first poem, "Ela", after becoming a contributor to the newspaper Marmota Fluminense, by Francisco de Paula Brito.
  • 1858 - He has French and Latin classes with Professor Father Antônio José da Silveira Sarmento. He becomes the proofreader of typography proofs and the bookstore of journalist Paula Brito, where he meets members of the Petalogical Society, such as Manuel Antônio de Almeida, Joaquim Manoel de Macedo. He collaborates in the newspaper O Paraíba, and in the Correio Mercantil.
  • 1866 - The comedy Os Deus de Casaca and its translation of the novel Os Trabalhadores do Mar, by Victor Hugo, are published. At the end of this year, Carolina Augusta Xavier de Novais, sister of poet Faustino Xavier de Novais and future wife of Machado, arrives in Rio de Janeiro.
  • 1867 - He is awarded the Order of the Rose, in the rank of knight, and is named assistant to the director of the Official Gazette.
  • 1868 - As an established critic, he guides the young poet Castro Alves in the world of letters, at the request of José de Alencar.
  • 1869 - He marries the Portuguese Carolina Augusta Xavier de Novais.
  • 1872 - Resurrection, his first novel, is published.
  • 1873 - He publishes Midnight Stories (stories) and "News from current Brazilian literature: instinct of nationality" (critical essay). He is appointed First Officer of the Secretary of State of the Ministry of Agriculture, Commerce and Public Works.
  • 1879 - He publishes in Revista Brasileira, the novel Memórias Póstumas de Brás Cubas and in A Estação, the novel Quincas Borba. In this also his study entitled "The new generation" is published.
  • 1881 - Memórias Póstumas de Brás Cubas is published as a book. He writes chronicles in the newspaper Gazeta de Notícias and takes on the role of Cabinet Officer for the Minister of Agriculture.
  • 1888 - He is elevated to officer of the Order of the Rose. He parades, on May 20, in the procession organized to celebrate Abolition
  • 1896 - Publishes Several Histories. Acclaimed, on December 15, to direct the first preparatory session for the foundation of the Brazilian Academy of Letters, he played a preponderant part in the creation of this institute, which he presides until his death.
  • 1904 - Publishes Esau and Jacob (novel). He goes to Friburgo in January with his sick wife. On October 20, Carolina died, days before their 35th wedding anniversary.
  • 1906 - Publishes Relíquias de Casa Velha (stories, criticism, theater).
  • 1908 - Publishes the Memorial de Aires (novel). He enters, on June 1, on sick leave. In the early hours of September 29, at 3:20 am, he died at his home, Rua Cosme Velho, 18; he is buried, according to his determination, in Carolina's grave, perpetual grave 1359, São João Batista Cemetery.

“Ao verme que primeiro roeu as frias carnes do meu cadáver dedico como saudosa lembrança estas memórias póstumas” (“To the worm that first gnawed the cold flesh of my corpse I dedicate these posthumous memories as a fond remembrance”)
